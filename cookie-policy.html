Cookie Usage Policy

By continuing to use our website, you confirm your agreement with the use of cookies as described in this policy. If you wish to opt out of cookie usage, you can adjust your browser settings accordingly.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They allow the site to remember your actions and preferences for a certain period, so you do not have to re-enter this information each time you visit. Cookies can be set by the site itself (first-party cookies) or by third parties (such as advertising platforms).

Typically, cookies contain the following information: the server name that created them, storage duration, and a unique identifier. Some cookies are essential for the site's functionality, while others help track user behavior for marketing and analytics purposes. There are temporary cookies (deleted when the browser is closed) and persistent cookies (stored for a set period or until manually deleted).

2. Types of Cookies We Use

We use different types of cookies to improve your experience. These perform various functions, from ensuring basic operability to personalizing content and analyzing user behavior.

  • Essential Cookies: Required for the proper functioning of the website, allowing navigation and access to secure areas.
  • Performance Cookies: Collect information on how you use the site (such as which pages you visit most) to improve functionality and performance.
  • Functional Cookies: Remember your settings, such as language, location, and login details, for a more convenient browsing experience.
  • Advertising Cookies: Used to display personalized ads based on your interests.
  • Social Media Cookies: Allow content sharing on social networks and track interactions with such content.
  • Analytical Cookies: Help collect data on site visits, traffic sources, and user behavior, enabling us to improve site structure and content.

3. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

  • Ensuring stable operation and improving site performance.
  • Remembering your preferences and login details.
  • Personalizing content and offers.
  • Analyzing user interactions to optimize the website.
  • Displaying targeted advertisements relevant to your interests.

4. Managing Cookies

You can manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers accept cookies by default, but you can change these settings to block or receive notifications when cookies are used. However, disabling cookies may limit some site functionalities.

5. Third-Party Cookies

We collaborate with trusted partners such as advertising networks, analytics platforms, and social media, which may use cookies to provide personalized content. We do not control third-party cookies, so we recommend reviewing their privacy policies.
